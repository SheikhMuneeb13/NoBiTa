\item{\textbf{Question 2.67:}}\\

\textit{The two equal sides of an isosceles triangle with fixed base b are decreasing at the rate of 3cm per second. How fast is the area decreasing when the two equal sides are equal to the base.}\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

\begin{centre}
\begin{tikzpicture}
\draw (0,0)--(8,0)--(4,4)--cycle;
\draw (4,0)--(4,4);
\node at (-0.3,-0.3){$A$};
\node at (8.3,-0.3){$B$};
\node at (4.3,4.3){$C$};
\node at (4,-0.3){$b$};
\node at (1.6,2){$l$};
\node at (6.4,2){$l$};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{centre}\\


Let the two sides of the isosceles triangle be $=l$.\\

$ Base=b=constant(given)$\\ 

Also,the rate at which the two sides are decreasing $=dl/dt=3cm$.\\

Now, By Pythagoras Theorem,\\
$

\hspace{1.3 cm}l^2=(b/2)^2+p^2\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}p= \sqrt{l^2-(b^2/4)}$\\

Now,\hspace{0.6 cm}Area of triangle $=A=1/2(p*b)$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}A $=1/2(\sqrt{l^2-b^2/4})*b$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$dA/dt=1/2*(b/2\sqrt{l^2-b^2/4}*2l(dl/dt))$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$dA/dt= (1/2)bl/\sqrt{l^2-b^2/4}*3$. \hspace{2 cm}(since $dl/dt=3 cm$)\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$dA/dt=(1/2)b^2/\sqrt{b^2-b^2/4}*3$. \hspace{3 cm}(as $l=b$)\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$dA/dt=(3/2)b*2/\sqrt{3}$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$dA/dt=\sqrt{3}*b cm^2/s$.\\

\item{\textbf{Question 2.68:}}\\

\textit{ A tank with rectangular base and rectangular sides, open at the top is to be constructed so that its depth is 2 m and volume is 8m^3. $If building of tank costs\\
Rs 70 per square meters for the base and Rs 4 per square meter for sides. What is the cost of least expensive tank.}$\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

Given, volume of tank $=8m^3$.\\

Depth of the tank $=2m$.\\

Cost of building the base of tank is Rs 70/sq mtrs and Rs 4/sqr mtr for building sides.\\

Let x, y and z be the length, breadth and depth of the tank respectively.\\

\therefore{} & \hspace{0.8 cm}Volume $=x*y*2=8m^3$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}Volume $=2*x*y=8$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$x*y=4$.\\
                                                                            
Now, Curved Area of tank $=A_c=2(2*x+2*y0)$.\\

 \hspace{0.8 cm}$A_c=2(2*x+2*y)$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$A_c=4(x+y)$.\\

\hspace{0.8 cm}$A_c=4(x+4/x)$. \hspace{3 cm}(since $y=4/x$)\\

Hence, Total cost of Curved sides $=4(x+4/x)*45=T_c$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$dT_c/dx=4(1-4/x^2)*45=0$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$x^2-y=0$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$x=\pm2$.\\

i.e,\hspace{0.8 cm}$x=2$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}$y=2$.\\

Hence, total cost $=(4*70+45*4(2+2))$.\\

or, \hspace{0.8 cm}total cost $=(280+360)$.\\
 
or, \hspace{0.8 cm}total cost $=Rs  640$.\\
